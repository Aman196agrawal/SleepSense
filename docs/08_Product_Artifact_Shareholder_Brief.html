Risks & Challenges

What Stands Between Us and the Goal — And How We Address It

🗂️

Training Data Scarcity High Risk

Diverse labelled snoring audio from Indian users (varied devices, rooms, positions) does not exist at the scale we need. Public datasets like ESC-50 and AudioSet were not built for this use case.

Mitigation: Transfer learning on pre-trained AudioSet weights + aggressive augmentation + user-feedback loop to label edge cases over time.
🍎

iOS Background Audio Restrictions High Risk

Apple aggressively limits background processes. A recording session killed mid-night would destroy user trust immediately.

Mitigation: Use AVAudioSession continuous-recording pattern (proven by SnoreLab). Watchdog timer alerts user if interrupted.
⚖️

Regulatory Exposure on Health Claims Medium Risk

Saying "you may have sleep apnea" is a medical claim. Getting it wrong risks user harm, legal liability, and potential FDA/CE classification if we expand globally.

Mitigation: Apnea screener is out of scope for MVP. Phase 2 introduces it with "Not a medical diagnosis" disclaimer, reviewed by a sleep medicine consultant.
📉

Post-First-Night User Churn Medium Risk

Sleep apps face extreme early drop-off. Without longitudinal data, the product's core value (trends over time) is invisible, creating a retention paradox.

Mitigation: Bedtime reminders, streak tracking, and weekly email summaries are in MVP scope. Engagement (DAU/MAU >30%) is a primary KPI from day one.
📡

Microphone Variability Across Devices Medium Risk

A ₹8,000 Android device mic is not the same as an iPhone 15 Pro. SNR and frequency response vary wildly across our target market's device mix.

Mitigation: Calibration step during onboarding (10s room-noise recording). Normalise to consistent loudness target before inference. Device metadata used as training feature.
☁️

Infrastructure Cost at Scale Low Risk

At 10,000 concurrent sessions, we process ~20,000 inference requests per minute. GPU time is not free, and miscalculating unit economics before pricing the subscription could compress margins.

Mitigation: Kafka smooths demand spikes. On-device TFLite offloads cost to user hardware. Profile cost-per-session before finalising subscription pricing.
